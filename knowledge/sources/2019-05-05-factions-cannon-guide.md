# Historical 1.12.2 factions cannoning guide

Source date: 2019-05-05
Original context: a community guide written to stop players using 1.8.8 cannons unchanged on 1.12.2.
Evidence level: `historical-hypothesis`.

## Concepts preserved

- Alignment and one-sided component placement to reduce lateral payload drift.
- One, two and three-stage boosters used to compact TNT or falling payloads.
- Main power as the launch engine.
- Hybrid payloads that attempt to overlap TNT and sand inside watered targets.
- Sand compressors, hammer timing, slab-bust timing and guiders.
- Repeater-path oddities where equal nominal delay built from different repeater layouts may not behave identically.
- Entity ordering, pseudo-nuking and the historical “tunnel effect.”
- Cannon naming by stack count, cycle time and special modules.

## Historical claims that must be tested, not trusted

- The guide uses “tick” for a 0.1-second redstone tick, not a 0.05-second game tick.
- The stated hammer-to-sand ratio of roughly 1:1.11 is a tuning hypothesis.
- “Y before X/Z” is a community simplification, not a universal engine contract.
- “Seven TNT busts seven slabs” is server, geometry and timing dependent.
- Entity ignition order, tunnel behavior and equal-delay repeater oddities require exact-version telemetry.
